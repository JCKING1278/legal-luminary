Eric Dane, the renowned actor known for his roles in "Grey's Anatomy" and "Euphoria," died at the age of 53 on February 19, 2026, due to complications from amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS). His representatives confirmed that he had publicly disclosed his diagnosis less than a year before his passing.

In a statement from his family, it was revealed that Dane spent his final moments surrounded by his close friends, wife, and two daughters, Billie and Georgia, who were described as the focal point of his life. The family requested privacy during this challenging time and expressed their gratitude for the outpouring of support from fans. They also emphasized Dane's dedication to raising awareness for ALS and his efforts to advocate for research into the disease, reflecting his desire to help others facing similar struggles.

Dane's career included several prominent roles, but he is best remembered for his portrayal of Dr. Mark Sloan on "Grey's Anatomy" and his character in the HBO series "Euphoria." His death represents a significant loss to the entertainment industry, where he was not only celebrated for his acting talent but also for his commitment to advocacy. The announcement of his passing has led to a wave of tributes from fans and colleagues, highlighting the impact he made and the legacy he leaves behind.
