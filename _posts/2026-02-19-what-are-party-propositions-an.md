AUSTIN, Texas — Some of the items on the ballots for both Democrats and Republicans this primary election season have caught the attention of a few Texans.

Voters can weigh in on party propositions, but what does that mean and are they actually binding?

The ballots don’t explain what the propositions do, so you might think you are voting on the Texas Constitution or voting on items to appear on the ballot in November’s midterm elections. But that’s not the case.

The yes-or-no questions are essentially opinion polls for the respective political parties, so they can get feedback on how their voters feel about particular issues.

The Democrats’ propositions focus on a range of issues, including health care, immigration, abortion rights, funding for public schools, voting rights, legalizing cannabis, raising the minimum wage, limiting gerrymandering and taxing the wealthy.
