Former Austin television news anchors Judy Maggio and Leslie Rhode, who once competed for viewers, have united to create a positive news platform called ATX Good News. The duo, who previously worked at KVUE and KXAN respectively, now co-anchor a video show that highlights uplifting stories from Central Texas. They recently marked the third anniversary of their initiative, which focuses on showcasing the efforts of local individuals and organizations making a difference in the community.

Maggio and Rhode's collaboration reflects a shift from traditional broadcasting to digital platforms, utilizing social media channels such as YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook to reach their audience. Their work aims to counterbalance the often negative narratives prevalent in news media by emphasizing positive community stories. This transition not only allows them to continue their passion for journalism but also fosters a sense of community engagement and support.

The significance of their project lies in its potential to inspire and uplift viewers, particularly in a time when many seek more optimistic content. As they continue to grow their platform, Maggio and Rhode hope to further connect with the Central Texas community and encourage others to share their own positive stories.
