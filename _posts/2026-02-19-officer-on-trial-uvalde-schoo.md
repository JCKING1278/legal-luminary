CORPUS CHRISTI, Texas — It's been three and a half years since a gunman entered Robb Elementary School in Uvalde, Texas, killing 19 students and two teachers. 

In the time since, there have been investigations into what happened, reports on its aftermath and court rulings on records requests relating to it all. 

Now, finally, one of the officers indicted in 2024 over law enforcement's response to the shooting is on trial. 

Uvalde school officer Adrian Gonzales is facing child endangerment and abandonment charges in a Corpus Christi court.  (Uvalde school police Chief Pete Arredondo was also indicted on those same charges. His trial date has not been set yet.)

KVUE will provide live gavel-to-gavel coverage of the entire process, from jury selection to potential sentencing.
