Republican Robert Brown seeks District 10 seat
Brown calls himself a corporate turnaround specialist with 20 years of business experience. He said that background makes him qualified to represent District 10
By Delaney Wolovlek
Published: Feb. 19, 2026 at 9:56 AM CST|Updated: 9 hours ago

BRAZOS COUNTY, Texas (KBTX) - A retail executive wants to bring corporate problem-solving to Congress. Republican Robert Brown is running for U.S. House District 10 on a platform of deregulation and reduced government spending.

Brown calls himself a corporate turnaround specialist with 20 years of business experience. He told KBTX that background makes him qualified to represent District 10, which covers Austin, the Brazos Valley, and parts of Southeast Texas.

“The idea of fixing problems without spending money sounds like something that we really need in Congress,” Brown said.

Addressing a Multi-Region District

Brown said District 10 needs a representative who understands its diversity. The district spans urban Austin, mid-sized towns like Huntsville, and growing communities near Houston’s airport.

Brown emphasized a consistent message: government overreach is the root cause of these problems across all communities.

“The biggest problem that we have in all of these areas—whether it’s urban, suburban, or rural—is the government being in the way of what they need,” he said.
