U.S. Representative Tony Gonzales is facing serious allegations regarding an affair with his deceased staffer, Regina Santos-Aviles, who died by self-immolation in September 2025. The Bexar County Medical Examiner's Office ruled her death a suicide, but following the incident, rumors about the nature of her relationship with Gonzales have emerged. While Gonzales has consistently denied any wrongdoing, he recently claimed to be a victim of blackmail related to these allegations.

Adrian Aviles, the husband of Santos-Aviles, has publicly accused Gonzales of having an affair with his wife. Aviles, represented by attorney Robert Barrera, has begun to vocalize these claims, which have gained traction as Gonzales prepares for a competitive Republican primary for his congressional seat. In a tweet, Gonzales shared a screenshot of what he described as a settlement offer from Aviles' attorney, indicating that he is being pressured for financial compensation concerning the allegations.

The situation poses significant implications for Gonzales's political future and raises potential legal issues stemming from the allegations. As the investigation progresses, the responses from Gonzales and the actions taken by Aviles and his legal team will likely influence the narrative surrounding this high-profile case and its impact on Gonzales's career.
