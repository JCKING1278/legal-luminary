By Jordan Vallender
Published: Feb. 18, 2026 at 7:21 PM CST|Updated: 24 hours ago

BRYAN, Texas (KBTX) - Twin City Mission is a community resource that provides help with dating and domestic violence.

February is Teen Dating Violence Awareness Month, and experts say one in three teenagers in romantic relationships admits to being in an unhealthy relationship, and teen dating violence often hides in plain sight.

Twin City Mission’s Director of Development, Lindsey Smart, says dating violence does not discriminate.

“It happens across all demographics – violence across any teen dating or domestic violence, it does not discriminate, unfortunately, so it happens across all demographics, ethnicities, and nationalities, but about 19% of teens will experience it,” Smart explained.

Four categories of dating violence

Smart says dating violence falls into four categories: emotional, physical, digital, and sexual.

“Digital, for instance, would be using social media or texting to bully or get somebody to do something. Some of the other ones – physical is obvious if they are exerting power and physically hurting or harming you, but it can also be verbal, so emotional, it can be using words and how they speak and interact with you,” Smart commented.
