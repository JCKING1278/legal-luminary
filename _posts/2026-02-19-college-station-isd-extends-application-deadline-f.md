College Station ISD extends application deadline for out-of-district transfer program
Planning to keep applications open to make a big push for students in May and June
KBTX News at Six Weekday EXTENDED(Recurring)
By Emily Paulson
Published: Feb. 19, 2026 at 2:09 PM CST|Updated: 5 hours ago

COLLEGE STATION, Texas (KBTX) - College Station ISD school board members announced they are extending the application deadline for its out-of-district transfer program. The application deadline was originally February 27th, but it will now remain open for the foreseeable future.

According to College Station ISD Superintendent Tim Harkrider, they’ve only received 15 applications so far.

The reasoning behind the extension is to allow more families to learn about the opportunity, as Harkrider claimed he met with realtors in Bryan last week who were unaware of the program.

The 2026-27 school year will be the program’s first year of implementation, with two select campuses for parents to choose from: Creek View Elementary and Cypress Grove Intermediate.

Harkrider said the first year of the program is the most difficult for recruiting students, and added that it will be easier in the future as more people become aware of it.

Administrators said they will keep applications open for the foreseeable future and will make a big push in May and June to recruit out-of-district transfers.
