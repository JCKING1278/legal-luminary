Texas Panhandle ranchers on edge as Lavender and 8 Ball fires burn 18,000+ acres
Two years after the Smokehouse Creek Fire devastated the region, dry waist-high grass and relentless winds are reigniting fear across the Texas Panhandle.
More Videos
Author: Adriana De Alba
Published: 9:26 PM CST February 18, 2026
Updated: 6:42 AM CST February 19, 2026

TEXAS, USA — Cattle rancher Shane Pennington was back in the saddle this week, moving cattle and lining up trailers in case evacuation orders come.

It is a routine he knows too well.

Wildfires are burning across the Texas Panhandle again, fueled by strong winds and critically dry conditions. A Red Flag warning is in effect in the region through Thursday as crews work to contain multiple fires.

The Texas A&M Forest Service said it responded to nine wildfires on Tuesday that burned more than 18,500 acres across the region.

Among the largest is the Lavender Fire in Oldham County, along U.S. Highway 385 just south of Tascosa. The fire is estimated at 12,000 acres and was about 10% contained as of Wednesday evening.

The 8 Ball Fire in Armstrong County, east of Goodnight, has burned an estimated 9,000 acres and is about 25% contained.

For ranchers like Pennington, the numbers are more than statistics. They are reminders.
