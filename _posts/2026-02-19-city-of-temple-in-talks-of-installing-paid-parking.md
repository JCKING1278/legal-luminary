City of Temple in talks of installing paid parking downtown
By Madison Herber
Published: Feb. 18, 2026 at 8:40 PM CST|Updated: 23 hours ago

TEMPLE, Texas (KWTX) - Residents in Temple could eventually have to start paying for parking to enjoy the downtown area.

Disclaimer: When KWTX talked to the city regarding this development, they referenced parking signs that were placed downtown as part of a long-term infrastructure plan. The city has since taken down those signs due to confusion about their purpose.

More than 1,500 people have signed a petition opposing paid parking downtown. The petition, started by owner of GreenDoor Pub on 2nd Street, claims the move could drive customers away.

Next door, Pignetti’s owner Clinton Harwell says change can be difficult but sometimes necessary.

“Anything that’s new in a community sometimes takes a little time to get adjusted to and I’m sure at some point they will be necessary,” Harwell explains.

For now, parking remains free. City leaders say the signage is part of a long-term plan to address growth and limited space.

“The signage that was installed reflects early preparation as we plan for the future of downtown but currently no paid parking system is in place,” says city spokesperson, Nohely Mackowiak.

Questions still remain, including how employees and parking garages would be affected. No final decisions have been made.
