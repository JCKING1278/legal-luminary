# Joe Jaworski and the 2026 Texas Attorney General race

**Joe Jaworski** (Democrat) is an attorney and former elected official from **Galveston, Texas**. In 2026 he is a candidate for **Attorney General of Texas**. He advanced from the March 3 Democratic primary and faces **Nathan Johnson** in the **Democratic primary runoff on May 26, 2026**. The **general election** is **November 3, 2026**. This page is informational only and does not endorse any candidate.

## Background and education

According to [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Joe_Jaworski), Jaworski resides in Galveston. He earned a bachelor’s degree in **Spanish language and literature** from **Davidson College** (1984) and a **J.D.** from the **University of Texas School of Law** (1991). Ballotpedia lists his career experience as including ownership of the **Jaworski Law Firm** and prior work with **Griggs and Harrison, P.C.**, and **Simpson, Beeton, Finegan & Jaworski, L.L.P.**

## Public service

Ballotpedia describes Jaworski as a **former mayor of Galveston**. His tenure and public priorities are summarized on [Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Joe_Jaworski) and discussed in his own [Candidate Connection](https://ballotpedia.org/Joe_Jaworski#Campaign_themes) responses.

## 2026 campaign for Attorney General

Jaworski is one of two Democrats in the **May 26, 2026 runoff** for attorney general, alongside **Nathan Johnson**, after both advanced from the March 3, 2026 primary (Ballotpedia reports Jaworski received about 26.4% in the first round of a three-way Democratic primary).[Ballotpedia](https://ballotpedia.org/Texas_Attorney_General_election,_2026) Stated campaign themes appear on his [campaign website](https://www.jaworskifortexas.com/) and in Ballotpedia’s survey responses.
